Essential Care Tips for Beginners

Master these three core principles and say goodbye to the 'plant killer' title

💧 1. The Golden Rule of Watering: 'Water when dry' +

Most indoor plants don't die from drought, but from root rot caused by overwatering. Don't water on a fixed schedule like 'every few days'. The correct method is: insert your finger about two knuckles deep into the soil, and only water thoroughly when it feels dry, until water flows out of the bottom. Always empty the water collected in the saucer!

☀️ 2. The Science of Light: Direct vs. Indirect +

Direct Light: Sunlight hits the leaves directly (like a south-facing windowsill), suitable for succulents and cacti.
Bright Indirect Light: 1-2 meters away from the window, or light filtered through a sheer curtain. This is the favorite environment for 80% of indoor foliage plants (like Monstera and Pothos).
Low Light Tolerant: Doesn't mean no light is needed, just that they can survive further from the window, like Snake Plants and Aglaonema.

🪴 3. Soil and Ventilation: The Overlooked Keys +

Never dig up garden soil from outside to pot indoor plants! Garden soil compacts easily and may contain pest eggs. Please buy pre-mixed indoor potting soil (usually containing peat moss, perlite, and coco coir) to ensure it is loose and breathable. Additionally, good ventilation greatly reduces the chances of plant diseases and pests.
